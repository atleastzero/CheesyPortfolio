Megan O'Bryan

From Megapedia, the free encyclopedia

Megan OBryan is currently an Applied Computer Science junior at Make School, located in San Francisco, CA. She has studied Software Engineering at Arizona State online and Embry-Riddle in Arizona, but favored the alternative start up style of Make School.

History

[edit]

O'Bryan was born and raised in Los Angeles. She went to high school at El Camino Real Charter High School, where she participated in NJROTC and a film program.

At El Camino, she took AP Chemistry, AP Calculus BC, AP US Government, and AP Computer Science. Dual enrolled at Pierce College, she continued her math studies, taking Calculus 3, Differential Equations, and Linear Algebra.

In her senior year, taking AP Computer Science, she discovered a passion for programming. She had a lot of passions, but spent the most time in programming club where she taught herself and her classmated concepts like prefix and postfix, assembly language, number base math, and logic.

She applied to a few majors, but ultimately it was always going to be Software Engineering at Embry-Riddle.
